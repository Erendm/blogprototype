// about us

Craft, community,
and clean code.

Bloom is a space for developers who believe that the how matters as much as the what — where ideas are explored with depth, and shared without noise.

Our origin story

Bloom started as a personal Notion doc in 2022 — a collection of hard-won patterns, late-night debugging sessions, and architecture decisions that either worked beautifully or failed spectacularly. Publishing it felt like the right thing to do.

What we publish

Long-form technical essays, reusable code snippets with real context, and project spotlights. Everything is edited for clarity. We favor depth over volume — a small number of genuinely useful things beats an endless feed.

Who writes here

A small rotating group of contributors — mostly working engineers, occasional researchers, and the occasional designer who actually knows what a closure is. Applications are open, but selective.

The tech stack

Lean and boring in the best way: static generation, edge caching, a real database for dynamic features, and zero cookie banners. The irony of not using the latest framework is not lost on us.

What we stand for

🌱

Depth over virality

We write for readers who slow down. Not for the algorithm.

🔍

Show your work

Every claim should be traceable. We link sources and share failures.

🤝

Community-first

The Projects section exists because great ideas deserve visibility.

🌸

Seasonal freshness

Content is reviewed and pruned. Old posts that mislead get flagged or removed.
